Printing Multicolor Stickers

Using the Roland 560i, I printed two multicolor stickers. My first sticker was a sticker of my dog. I imported his image into adobe photoshop and copied it into corel draw. Around his head, I had to put a specific pink outline so that the printer knew where to cut. My second sticker was a picture of my uncles face. I inserted it into a circle then put the pink outline around it so it would be cut out. Next, I loaded them onto a flashdrive and printed them.


Using the Roland GS-24 CAMM 1, I printed two one color stickers. My first sticker was a yellow dog and the second was a pink elephant. I used Corel draw and uploaded clipart of the dog and elephant. I then printed them.


Why did I create each sticker?

I made the sticker of my dog face because I love him. I made the sticker of my uncle for my father. I made the elephant and yellow dog sticker because I like dogs and elephants.

What was the thought process while I was creating my notebook?

I put the stickers on my laptop instead of a notebook. My thought process was that I wanted to have quality stickers on my laptop.

What was the most challenging part of this process?

The most challenging part was getting the pink boarder around the images.

What did you learn in the process?

I learned how to insert images into shapes and make stickers.

Molding and Casting Pewter

For this assignment, I made an Indians pendant. First, using Corel Draw, I made my wooden molds. Using the Laser cutter, I engraved the top wooden block with a simple circle. On the middle wood block, I cut a hole so I could pore the pewter in. The bottom block had the Cleveland Indians logo on it. After I made the wooden molds, I clamped them together and was able to pored in baby powder, so the pewter would not stick and then the pewter. When the pewter cooled, I took it out of the mold and cut off the top excess pewter, buffed it out, and drilled a hole in the top.
